\subsection{Summary}\label{subsec:application_summary}

The Unity application closes the loop the pipeline opened. \texttt{ManoLiveReceiver}
decodes the streamed datagram back into two posed and placed hand skeletons,
reversing the serialisation of~\autoref{subsec:streaming} step for step, and exposes
that skeleton as a shared resource. On top of it, one gesture vocabulary, one
body-anchored menu, and one pointing component drive four scenes that use the hands
in markedly different ways: a character that is by turns a companion and a grappling
avatar, a mesh deformed vertex by vertex, a city flown through, rearranged, and
traversed on grappling gear, and a painting tool whose drawings leave the
application as point clouds for reconstruction.

Across all four, the recurring observation is the one the state of the art
frames~(\autoref{subsec:sota_interaction}): each interaction is of a kind that
comparable systems deliver only with a peripheral, a head-mounted display, or a
controller, and each is achieved here from a single webcam and a commodity
\acrshort{cpu}, with the user holding and wearing nothing but, optionally, a haptic
vest. The evaluation that follows in~\autoref{sec:evaluation} confirms that the loop
is not merely closed but fast and cheap enough for these interactions to feel
immediate.
