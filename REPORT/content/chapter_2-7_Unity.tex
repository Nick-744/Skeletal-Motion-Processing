\subsection{Unity 3D}\label{subsec:unity}

Unity is a cross-platform game engine developed by Unity Technologies.
The engine supports the creation of both three-dimensional and
two-dimensional games, as well as interactive simulations and
visualisations.
It is widely adopted across the industry due to its broad platform
compatibility, enabling developers to target a diverse range of devices
including personal computers, gaming consoles, mobile devices, and
\acrshort{vr}/\acrshort{ar} headsets.

Unity's primary scripting interface is C\#, supported through the Mono
runtime, and is used both within the Unity editor --- in the form of
plugins and custom tools --- and within applications themselves.
This scripting layer grants developers fine-grained control over scene
logic, physics, input handling, and rendering behaviour.
The engine also provides drag-and-drop scene composition tools,
making it accessible to both technical and non-technical creators.
