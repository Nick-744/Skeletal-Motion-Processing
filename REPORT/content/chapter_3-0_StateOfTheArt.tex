\section{State of the Art}\label{sec:sota}

The previous chapter established the theoretical foundations on which this
system rests, deriving the specific models and algorithms it employs.
This chapter takes a wider view: rather than explaining how each component
works, it surveys the landscape of competing approaches to the problems the
system solves and positions the present work within it.
The literature on markerless hand capture, articulated pose estimation, and
hand-driven interaction is extensive; the discussion is therefore deliberately
bounded by the setting of this thesis, and prior work is examined only insofar
as it bears on that setting.

That setting is defined by three constraints first introduced in
\autoref{subsec:gap} --- monocular RGB input, CPU-only real-time compute, and
off-the-shelf hardware accessibility --- which together serve as the lenses
through which all surveyed work is judged.
Much of the most accurate prior work assumes depth input, GPU-class compute, or
head-mounted multi-camera hardware; such methods are not dismissed, but they
are weighed against the adoption cost they would impose on a typical user.
