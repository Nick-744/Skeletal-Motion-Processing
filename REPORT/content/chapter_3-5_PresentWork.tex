\subsection{Positioning the Present Work}\label{subsec:sota_positioning}

The four preceding subsections each judged a body of prior work against the
three constraints of~\autoref{subsec:gap}.
Taken individually, several of the surveyed systems satisfy one or two of those
constraints.
Taken together, what emerges is that no single prior system satisfies all three
at once while also producing the canonical pose an interactive avatar requires.
This subsection draws those threads together and states precisely what occupying
that operating point demands, and what the present work contributes by occupying
it.
The contribution claimed here is not a new algorithm --- the components are drawn
from existing work --- but the specific combination that the survey leaves open,
and the demonstration that it suffices.

The clearest tension runs through the estimation
literature~(\autoref{subsec:sota_estimation}).
The model-free detectors --- MediaPipe~\cite{zhang2020mediapipehands} and the
weakly-supervised regressor of Spurr et al.~\cite{spurr2020weakly} --- emit only
joint coordinates, with no rotations, no kinematic hierarchy, and no scale
invariance~(\autoref{subsubsec:model-free}), and so cannot on their own drive a
rigged skeleton; MediaPipe further demonstrates that this class can run at
interactive rate on commodity hardware.
The model-based regressors --- Minimal-Hand~\cite{zhou2020monocular},
HandOccNet~\cite{park2022handoccnet}, and HaMeR~\cite{pavlakos2024hamer} ---
recover exactly the canonical \acrshort{mano} output that is missing, but reach
it only on \acrshort{gpu}-class hardware where they report timing at all; two of
the three report neither inference cost nor
hardware~(\autoref{subsubsec:model-based}).
MobRecon~\cite{chen2022mobrecon} is the one surveyed mesh-recovery method to
combine CPU-rate operation with accessible hardware, yet it does so by
abandoning the parametric representation altogether: its directly predicted mesh
vertices carry no joint rotations, and therefore cannot drive a skeleton without
a separate pose-extraction stage~(\autoref{subsubsec:sota_hardware}).
The parametric-model survey marks the same boundary from the other side ---
\acrshort{mano} is adopted precisely because its lightweight forward pass meets
the CPU constraint, where the anatomically richer NIMBLE~\cite{zhang2022nimble}
does not~(\autoref{subsec:sota_parametric}).
Across the estimation literature, then, canonical \acrshort{mano} joint rotations
and interactive CPU-only operation from a single webcam are delivered by
different systems, never by the same one.

The present work reaches that point not by accelerating a learned regressor ---
a direction no surveyed method currently supports at interactive rate on
\acrshort{cpu} while emitting \acrshort{mano}-compatible rotations --- but by
removing the learned component from the pose path entirely.
It pairs model-free MediaPipe detection with the analytical AIK
solver~\cite{meng2021aik}, which the inverse-kinematics survey singled out as the
only one of four solver families that is simultaneously data-free, deterministic
in per-frame cost, and runnable on
\acrshort{cpu}~(\autoref{subsubsec:sota_ik_summary}).
Because the 21-landmark input is fully observed, the landmark-to-rotation map is
closed-form~(\autoref{subsec:aik}): the canonical pose follows from a single
\acrshort{svd}-based point-set alignment and one swing-only traversal of the
kinematic tree, with no convergence loop, no training-data dependency, and no
\acrshort{gpu}.
This is the combination the survey leaves open, and the one the remainder of the
thesis implements.

This is a trade rather than a free gain, and the survey was explicit about its
price.
Setting the twist of every finger joint to zero~(\autoref{subsec:aik}) discards
the degree of freedom that a learned solver such as
HybrIK-X~\cite{li2023hybrikx} is able to recover from image
features~(\autoref{subsubsec:sota_ik_neural}), so the present system does not
approach the reconstruction accuracy that defines the upper bound of that work.
As~\autoref{subsubsec:sota_ik_summary} put it, HybrIK-X marks the accuracy
ceiling this thesis does not reach, and AIK the latency and hardware floor within
which it operates.
What the choice buys is the operating point itself --- a per-frame cost bounded
by construction and independent of pose complexity, initialisation, or hardware
class --- and it is the occupation of that point, not any gain in accuracy, that
distinguishes the system from the regressors
of~\autoref{subsec:sota_estimation}.

From that operating point follow two consequences that no surveyed system jointly
demonstrates.
The first is empirical: the assembled pipeline is not merely CPU-runnable in
principle but responsive in practice, with a measured mean software latency of
\SI{30.68}{\ms} over more than \num{20000} frames on a laptop \acrshort{cpu}, and
no \acrshort{gpu} at any stage~(\autoref{subsec:contributions}) --- comfortably
within the \SI{60}{\ms} perceptibility threshold
of~\autoref{subsec:one_euro_filter}, and a level of responsiveness the
model-based regressors attain only on a \acrshort{gpu}.
The second is interactional: the recovered canonical pose is shown to be
sufficient to drive four distinct
applications~(\autoref{subsec:contributions}) --- a physics-based grappling
interaction, a spring-physics face deformer, an editable city, and a 3D painting
tool with point-cloud export.
The last of these is the sharpest point of departure.
The immersive creation tools it parallels ---
CavePainting~\cite{keefe2001cavepainting} and its commercial successors ---
deliver spatial drawing only behind a head-mounted display and a pair of
controllers~(\autoref{subsubsec:sota_creative}), and the production
hand-tracking systems that might replace those controllers still presuppose a
dedicated peripheral or a head-mounted multi-camera
rig~(\autoref{subsubsec:sota_immersive}).
The present system offers the same core affordance --- depositing geometry in
three dimensions by moving the hand --- from the single commodity webcam and
CPU-only pipeline used throughout, removing the input apparatus every comparable
system assumes.
That conjunction --- a canonical \acrshort{mano} pose recovered at interactive
rate on accessible hardware, and put to fine-grained interactive and creative use
with no sensing apparatus beyond a webcam --- is what the present work
contributes, and what no system surveyed in this chapter offers complete.
